Modern Clients, Modern Problems

Dear Coaches, Therapists, Counsellors, Psychotherapists, and whoever this resonates with: Does your website pass the 3-step test? Most don't, and it's costing you more clients than you think.

Dear Coaches, Therapists, Counsellors, Psychotherapists, and whoever this resonates with:

Does your website pass the 3-step test? Most don't, and it's costing you more clients than you think.

The 3-Step Test

Step 1: Can a stranger understand what you do in under 5 seconds?
Look at your homepage. Cover the navigation. Read only the headline. Does it immediately tell someone who you help and how? If not, you're losing people before they even start reading.

Step 2: Is there a clear next step?
After reading your headline, is it obvious what to do next? One button. One direction. Most sites have three competing calls to action, or none at all. Both cost you clients.

Step 3: Does it feel like you?
This one is the hardest to quantify, but clients feel it immediately. Your site should carry the same energy as being in a session with you - warm, professional, trustworthy. If it looks like a template from 2015, it's sending a message you probably don't want to send.

Why This Matters More Than You Think

In 2026, your website is your first session. Before someone books with you, before they read your bio, before they even decide if they like your rates - they have already formed an opinion based on how your site looks and feels.

According to Stanford research, 75% of people judge a business's credibility based on its website design. That's not a small number. That's three out of four potential clients making a decision before they read a single word you've written.

And here's what makes this particularly relevant for therapists, counsellors, and coaches: your clients are often already in a vulnerable state when they find you. They are looking for someone who feels safe, professional, and aligned with their values. A messy, outdated, or generic site doesn't just lose you business - it can genuinely put people off reaching out at all.

What a Modern Client Expects

Modern clients - especially in the mental health and coaching space - are more discerning than ever. Here's what they're looking for, often without even knowing it:

  • Speed. If your site takes more than 3 seconds to load, 40% of visitors leave. This isn't an exaggeration - it's Google's own data.
  • Mobile design. Over 70% of people searching for therapists or coaches are on their phone. If your site breaks on mobile, you're invisible to the majority of your potential clients.
  • Authenticity. Stock photos of people smiling at laptops don't convert. Real photography, your real words, your real story - this is what builds trust.
  • Simplicity. The less a potential client has to think, the more likely they are to reach out. Clean design, clear copy, obvious next steps.

The Problem With DIY Solutions

I'm not saying you need to spend thousands of pounds on a web agency. But I am saying that the Wix or Squarespace template you put together in a weekend is probably working against you.

Not because those platforms are bad. They're fine tools. The problem is that without a background in conversion design, user experience, or branding - most people build sites that look okay but don't convert. They tick the box of having a website, but the site isn't actually doing any work for them.

I know this because I built websites for businesses before I became a coach. I've seen both sides. And I've watched talented, deeply skilled practitioners lose clients to less qualified competitors simply because the competitor's site felt more trustworthy.

What You Can Do About It

Start with the 3-step test above. Be honest with yourself. Ask a friend or colleague who doesn't know your work to look at your homepage and tell you what they think you do within 5 seconds.

Then look at your mobile experience. Pull your site up on your phone right now. Does it look the same? Does the text fit? Do the buttons work? Is the booking link easy to find?

If you find problems - and most people do - don't panic. These are fixable. But they do need to be fixed, because in a world where clients have more choices than ever, your online presence is the first filter they apply.

Your clients deserve to find you easily. And you deserve a site that reflects the quality of the work you do.
