Modern Clients, Modern Problems

Dear Coaches, Therapists, Counsellors, Psychotherapists, and whoever this resonates with: Does your website pass the 3-step test? Most don't, and it’s costing you more clients than you think.

Who are your clients? Who are you trying to provide a service for? What is your niche? Unfortunately, this matters more than you think when it comes to YOU and your online presence.

We live in a world where our clients WILL put our name into Google, ChatGPT, Gemini, and other search engines. I do it, YOU do it, and your clients do it. There is no question about that part.

If we can agree on all of that, then please tell me: why does your website look the way it does? Yes, YOUR website specifically. I search and scroll through hundreds of sites each week as a hobby, and it is extremely rare to find a practice that has actually paid the slightest bit of attention to the way they show up. And no - having a website is not better than not having one.

“Reality check”

A rubbish site will actively turn your clients away. If you own one and you are so proud of it because you have done it all by yourself, please make sure you ask for an opinion from the right people, not your inner circle.

The website is the core, the absolute centre of your practice. It can be everything you wish and want: the main client route to find you, the contract generator, the form collector, the practice booking system, the payment collection method, the reminders, and even a room booking system (yes, I can do that)... the list is endless, really. But your site isn't doing any of that, is it? Instead, so many people - likely yourself included - end up paying for directories just to be included in a never-ending list and somehow stay visible because their website has become an afterthought.

LET’S CHANGE THIS. Let’s make our sites presentable, professional, unique, and beautiful, and most importantly, let’s attract the right people! All of that is easier than you think, and crazy enough, it’s all possible for FREE without any monthly subscriptions, retainers, or anything like that.

By having a truly presentable website that works and does what’s intended, you show that your clients matter, that they are not an afterthought, and it gives them many ways they can connect to you and be part of the journey. A website isn’t just for you; it is for them, too.

“Are you proud to give your website as a gift to your client? If not, there is work to be done.”

Especially if your niche is ADHD, LGBT, Depression, or Anxiety... more often than not, your website works as a deterrent.

Take my advice: you don’t have to do it with me, but please DO look into it, as let’s pay attention to our online presence a little bit more.

The "Is My Website Helping?" Checklist

Be honest. Look at your site and answer these three questions:

1. Does it load under 3 seconds?

  • Fact: Over 70% of potential clients will bounce if your site doesn't load instantly.

2. Is it clear who you help? (In under 5 seconds?)

  • Fact: Users form an opinion about your website in 0.05 seconds; if they don't know what you do immediately, they leave.

3. Is it easy to book you? (Right now?)

- Fact: Every extra click between a client and your booking calendar reduces the chance of them actually booking by 20%.

If you answered "No" to any of these, or aren't sure - we need to talk.
