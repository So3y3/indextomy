Identity Before Habits

You don't rise to your goals you fall to your identity. Until you shift how you see yourself, habits are just performances.

The Identity Gap

Most habit advice focuses on behaviour. Do this. Track that. Build a streak.

It misses the deeper layer: who you believe yourself to be.

The Athlete Principle

Ask someone who exercises consistently why they do it. They won't say "because of my habit tracker." They'll say some version of: I'm someone who moves their body.

The behaviour flows from the identity. Not the other way around.

How to Shift Identity

Identity shifts come through evidence. Small, consistent actions that prove to yourself you are the person you want to become.

  • You don't try to read more you become someone who reads
  • You don't try to eat well you become someone who takes their health seriously
  • You don't try to be productive you become someone who follows through

The coaching work is in accelerating this identity evolution removing the counterevidence (the exceptions, the excuses) and stacking the proof.

The Coaching Conversation

In my practice, the first sessions are rarely about habits. They're about unpacking the story you're telling about who you are and what you're capable of.

That's where the real work begins.
